The Problem

Hydrogen has not worked

Current hydrogen solutions rely on building out entirely new hydrogen infrastructure, which is costly, complex, and slow. The challenge isn’t hydrogen itself—it’s getting it where it needs to go, efficiently and affordably. Until that’s solved, adoption will remain out of reach for most real-world applications.

Hydrogen

Today, hydrogen must be stored as a cryogenic liquid or compressed gas—making it expensive to move and handle.

02

The Solution

A safer, easier hydrogen fuel

We use Liquid Organic Hydrogen Carriers (LOHCs) that safely locks hydrogen into a stable, room-temperature liquid. It's non-flammable, easy to handle, and compatible with existing diesel infrastructure—making hydrogen as practical and cost-effective as today’s fuels.

Hydrogenation

Transportation

LOHCs are organic molecules that can be reversibly hydrogenated and dehydrogenated to store and release hydrogen.

Because LOHCs have similar physical properties to diesel, they are compatible with existing diesel infrastructure including pipelines and pumps.

03

The Key

Our technology

Our Hydrogen Release Unit is the key to extracting hydrogen from LOHCs—safely and efficiently, right on board. Whether in a truck, ship, or other heavy-duty platform, our technology enables a practical and cost-effective path to zero emissions.

Hydrogen Release Unit

Engine

Fuel Cell

Hydrogen-rich LOHC enters our Hydrogen Release Unit, where hydrogen is extracted on demand. The hydrogen can then power an engine or fuel cell, while the used liquid is sent back to the tank—ready to be reused.

We can extract hydrogen at optimal pressures and flow rates to power a hydrogen internal combustion engine.

We can extract hydrogen to power a hydrogen fuel cell for complete electric operation.
